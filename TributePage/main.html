Steve Robert Irwin

Australian wildlife expert in the protection, conservation and relocation of animals around the world, also the television personality known as the "Crocodile Hunter". He was a hero and inspiration to millions around the world.

Here is a time line of The Crocodile Hunters life:

  • 1962 - Born in Upper Ferntree Gully, Melbourne
  • 1970 - moved to Queensland where his parents started the Queensland and Fauna Park
  • 1971 - - Began handling reptiles and wrestling Crocodiles
  • 1979 - Graduated from High School and became a crocodile trapper
  • 1991 - meets his soon to be wife, Terri
  • 1992 - Takes over his parents' Wilflife Park and renames it 'The Australia Zoo' - Marries Terri
  • 1996 - 'The Crocodile Hunter' hits the air for the first time on T.V.
  • 1998 - He creates another T.V. program called 'The Ten Deadliest Snakes in the World'
  • 1998 - The Birth of his First child 'Bindi'
  • 2001 - He was awarded the Centenary Medal for his 'Service to Global Conservation and to Australian Tourism'
  • 2002 - wildlife Warriors were formed, a conservation foundation originaly called the 'Steve Irwin Conservation', dedicated to education of people in the protection of injured, threatened and endangered wildlife.
  • 2002 - Steve stars in his first movie, 'The Crocodile Hunter: Collision Course'
  • 2003 - His second son 'Robert' was born
  • 2006 - Irwim was fatally pierced in the chest by a stingray whislt snorkeling at teh Great Barrier Reef. Irwin was in the area filming his own documentary 'Ocean's Deadliest'
